## Introduction

Technological advances are rapidly evolving water prediction capabilities. From machine learning breakthroughs to advances in computational infrastructure, the potential for accurate hydrologic forecasting has never been greater.

But how do these research innovations transition into real-world operational systems?

The answer is the **NextGen Research DataStream (NRDS)**.

Developed by Lynker and the Alabama Water Institute under the CIROH partnership, NRDS is designed to transform research ideas into scalable, deployable numerical prediction systems.

---

## What is NRDS?

The NRDS is an **automated numerical prediction system** that generates regular streamflow forecasts using:

- **NextGen Water Resources Modeling Framework (NextGen)** as the modeling engine  
- **NextGen In A Box (NGIAB)** as the simulation environment  

It operates across the contiguous United States (CONUS) on the **R2O Hybrid Cloud (R2OHC)** platform deployed on AWS.

At its core, NRDS enables:

- Rapid deployment of research ideas as “datastreams”  
- Iterative improvement through evaluation and refinement  
- Seamless transition from research to operational adoption  

The entire system is:

- Open source  
- Reproducible  
- Publicly accessible  
- Community-editable  

---

## Why NRDS Matters

Historically, operational hydrologic modeling has been limited to closed systems managed by federal agencies.

While research is published, the simulations themselves are often difficult to reproduce or extend, slowing innovation.

NRDS changes this paradigm by enabling:

- Full transparency in modeling workflows  
- Open access to data, configurations, and outputs  
- Faster research-to-operations (R2O) transitions  

Deployed via AWS, NRDS continuously:

1. Ingests meteorological forcing data  
2. Processes it through NextGen  
3. Generates nationwide streamflow predictions  

All components, including:

- Forcing data  
- Model configurations  
- Infrastructure  
- Simulation outputs  

are publicly available through the NRDS data portal and visualizer.

This creates a **community-driven feedback loop**, where improvements can directly influence operational-like systems.

## How the Community Can Contribute

NRDS is built around a simple and powerful contribution model.

Researchers can:

1. Improve parameterizations or model configurations  
2. Submit updates via the GitHub Issues page  
3. Provide supporting evidence and validation  

Once reviewed and approved, contributions are:

- Merged into the system  
- Deployed into live NRDS workflows  

This process was demonstrated at **CIROH DevCon 2025**, where participants successfully:

- Ran simulations  
- Evaluated outputs  
- Submitted improvements  

All within a single session.

NRDS is not a centrally controlled product — it is a platform designed to amplify the expertise of the hydrologic community.

## Multiple Datastreams and Scalability

NRDS supports multiple concurrent datastreams, each representing different modeling approaches.

### Current Datastreams

- **CFE-NOM Datastream**
  - Combines Conceptual Functional Equivalent (CFE) model with Noah-OWP-Modular  
  - Runs across all CONUS, parallelized by VPUs  

- **LSTM Datastream**
  - Machine learning-based streamflow prediction  
  - Deployed in production (v2.2.0, February 2026)  

- **Routing-Only Datastream**
  - Focused on T-Route channel routing  
  - Currently applied to VPU 03W  

These datastreams demonstrate NRDS’s ability to scale and integrate diverse modeling approaches.

## The Broader Ecosystem

NRDS is part of a larger ecosystem of CIROH tools:

- **NGIAB** → Containerized runtime for local simulation  
- **DataStreamCLI** → End-to-end workflow automation  
- **ForcingProcessor** → Converts meteorological data into NextGen inputs  
- **TEEHR** → Evaluation and performance analysis tools  

### End-to-End Workflow

1. Prepare data using ForcingProcessor and DataStreamCLI  
2. Run simulations locally with NGIAB  
3. Evaluate outputs using TEEHR  
4. Contribute improvements back to NRDS  

This creates a **complete, reproducible pipeline** from research to deployment.

## What’s Next

NRDS continues to evolve toward a more powerful operational system.

Key developments include:

- Integration of **δHBV 2.0 MTS** (hourly differentiable model)  
- Expansion of machine learning-based hydrologic models  
- Enhanced accessibility through:
  - NRDS Visualizer  
  - TEEHR Evaluation Dashboard  

These tools enable real-time evaluation and comparison of model performance across datastreams.
